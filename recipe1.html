Butter Chicken Recipe

Ingredients:

For the Chicken Marinade:

  • 500g Boneless Chicken, cut into cubes
  • 1 cup Greek yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons garlic, minced
  • 2 teaspoons ginger, minced
  • 2 teaspoons Kashmiri chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon kasthuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • Salt, to taste

For the Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 3/4 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 4 cardamoms
  • 4 cloves
  • 1 cup tomato puree
  • 1/4 cup cashews, blended into a paste
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine Greek yogurt, lemon juice, minced garlic, minced ginger, Kashmiri chili powder, garam masala, coriander powder, cumin powder, kasthuri methi, and salt. Mix well.
  2. Add the chicken cubes to the marinade and ensure they are well coated. Cover and let it marinate for at least 20 minutes. For best results, marinate for a few hours or overnight in the refrigerator.
  3. In a pan, heat 2 tablespoons of butter over medium heat. Add ginger-garlic paste and saut√© until fragrant, about 1-2 minutes.
  4. Add the slit green chilies, cardamoms, and cloves, cooking for another minute to release their flavors.
  5. Add the tomato puree and cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally until the raw smell disappears.
  6. Stir in the cashew paste and cook for another 5 minutes. This will thicken the sauce.
  7. Now, add the marinated chicken along with any leftover marinade. Cook for 10-12 minutes until the chicken is cooked through.
  8. Reduce the heat to low, then add the heavy cream and sugar. Stir well and let it simmer for 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  9. Finally, sprinkle the remaining garam masala and kasthuri methi on top. Stir and simmer for another 2 minutes.
  10. Serve hot with naan, roti, or steamed rice, garnished with fresh coriander if desired.

Tips:

  • For extra flavor, try adding a pinch of smoked paprika or a dash of liquid smoke to mimic a tandoori flavor.
  • Adjust the spice levels according to your preference by varying the amount of chili powder and green chilies.
  • For a richer sauce, you can add more cream or a tablespoon of cashew nuts blended into the sauce.
