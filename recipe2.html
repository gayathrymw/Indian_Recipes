Malabar Biriyani Recipe

Ingredients:

For the Chicken Marinade:

  • 500g chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander and mint leaves, chopped (a handful of each)

For the Rice:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 4 cups water
  • 4-5 whole cloves
  • 2-3 green cardamoms
  • 1-2 bay leaves
  • 1-2 cinnamon sticks
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • Salt to taste

For Layering:

  • 3-4 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • Fried onions (store-bought or homemade)
  • Boiled eggs (optional, for garnishing)
  • Fresh coriander and mint leaves, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, green chilies, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, lemon juice, and salt. Mix well.
  2. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade, ensuring they are well coated. Cover and let it marinate for at least 1 hour (preferably overnight in the refrigerator).
  3. Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak it in water for 30 minutes, then drain.
  4. In a large pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Add whole cloves, cardamoms, bay leaves, cinnamon sticks, cumin seeds, and salt.
  5. Add the soaked and drained rice to the boiling water. Cook until the rice is 70% done (it should still have a bite to it). Drain the rice and set aside.
  6. In a large, heavy-bottomed pot, heat ghee or oil over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and saut√© until golden brown.
  7. Add the marinated chicken along with the marinade. Cook for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is browned and cooked through.
  8. Reduce the heat to low. Spread half of the partially cooked rice over the chicken in an even layer.
  9. Sprinkle half of the fried onions, chopped coriander, and mint leaves over the rice.
  10. Add the remaining rice and layer it with the remaining fried onions, coriander, and mint. You can also place boiled eggs on top if using.
  11. Drizzle a little ghee on top if desired.
  12. Cover the pot tightly with a lid. You can seal it with dough or a clean kitchen towel to trap the steam.
  13. Cook on low heat for 30-40 minutes to allow the flavors to meld and the rice to finish cooking.
  14. Once done, gently fluff the biryani with a fork. Serve hot, garnished with additional fried onions and fresh herbs, along with raita or a salad on the side.
